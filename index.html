My journey

Born and raised in Uganda, my journey in technology exemplifies a unique pioneering spirit and an unwavering commitment to innovation. From winning coding challenges to creating immersive XR experiences, I have consistently embraced every opportunity to learn and grow. Now, as a Junior Designer at IA Interior Architects and a Software Engineer at Exonicus Inc., I channel my unique background and competitive nature into every project. Driven by the belief that technology can enhance education, health, and productivity, I founded SableSync to create impactful XR solutions. My roles have spanned from developing AR applications at Snap Inc. to teaching the first-ever AR/VR course at AVELA University of Washington, always with a focus on innovation and transformative impact. I am a dedicated student of leadership and human-centered engineering, continuously exploring how people connect with technology. I thrive in the chaos of learning and unlearning, embracing change with vigor and applying my insights to create meaningful, user-focused experiences.

Story telling

RevereXR Hackathon

This project aims to leverage Steve Sneed’s experiences with The Langston Hughes Performing Arts Institute in Seattle’s Central District. Steve’s history is strongly connected to the Langston. As a teenager, Steve was part of an African dance and drumming group (the ogandes) that performed multiple times at the Langston. Steve went to college, he learned about theater direction, after that, he was hired by the City of Seattle to actually run Langston! So what he created was a cultural system, a framework that helped young people navigate challenges and define their identities.

  • Client: RevereXR Hackathon
  • Category: Immersive Story telling
